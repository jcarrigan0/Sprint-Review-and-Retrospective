# Q and A
Q: How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
A: I first break down the user’s needs into simpler parts so they can be implemented step by step. I then determine the intended result so that I can establish what is considered a “success.” User stories essentially do this for me. They clearly explain intended implementations and describe conditions for success.

Q: How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
A: I approach developing programs by breaking them into sections, categorizing tasks based on importance, and grouping them by functionality. In the future, when I’m working on a team, I hope to incorporate Scrum meetings and daily stand-ups. This process provides multiple perspectives on the same issue and greatly assists in developing a program.

Q: What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
A: Being a good team member in software development is much more than being a good coder. It requires transparency, strong communication, and a clear understanding of your role. Being able to see the team as a whole instead of having tunnel vision on your own tasks is a key factor in achieving success in software development.
